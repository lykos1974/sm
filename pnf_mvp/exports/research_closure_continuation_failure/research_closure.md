# Research Closure: Continuation-Failure Reversal Branch

## Scope
Hypothesis investigated: a **FAILED LONG + DEEP** structural outcome can produce a profitable future **SHORT WATCH / reversal continuation** execution edge.

## Research phases (summary)
- Structural reversal scans across continuation-failure state transitions.
- Watch promotion analysis from structural state into short-watch candidates.
- Execution realism validation under executable trigger/exit assumptions.
- Same-timestamp causality audit for opposite-state coexistence contamination.
- Artifact exclusion reruns to test persistence after data/label artifact controls.
- Larger MEXC pilot validation for scale and cross-sample robustness.

## Key findings
- Structural polarity instability is present in continuation-failure regimes.
- Same-side TP2 continuation probability collapses after **FAILED LONG + DEEP** states.
- Same-timestamp opposite-state coexistence materially distorts candidate datasets.
- Artifact filtering reduced contamination but did not fully remove observed reversal behavior.
- The larger pilot failed execution realism validation.

## Final execution conclusion
- Hypothesis is rejected as a robust execution edge.
- Observed effects did not generalize on the larger dataset.
- Overlap contamination and small-sample fragility remain severe.
- No promotion to strategy or execution phase.

## What remains valid
- Valid as a structural observation only.
- Potential regime-instability signal at state-transition level.
- Usable only for future structural-state research.

## What not to do next
- No further optimizer mining on this branch.
- No threshold-relaxation attempts to recover edge.
- No production deployment.
- No alpha claims derived from this branch.

## Future reopen conditions
Reopen only if all of the following are met:
- Much larger labeled datasets.
- Broader symbol coverage.
- Stable positive execution realism that survives holdout validation.
